## Instructions

1. **Clarify the research question** using PICO/PICOS framework
   - If ambiguous, ask for clarification before proceeding

2. **Recommend study design** with justification:
   - Why this design over alternatives?
   - Consider feasibility, ethics, timeline, budget constraints

3. **Define study population**:
   - Inclusion criteria (specific, measurable)
   - Exclusion criteria (justified, not overly restrictive)
   - Target population vs. study sample

4. **Specify endpoints**:
   - Primary endpoint: one, clearly defined, clinically meaningful
   - Secondary endpoints: limited number, pre-specified
   - Exploratory endpoints: clearly labeled as hypothesis-generating
   - Measurement method and timing for each

5. **Describe the intervention and comparison**:
   - Dosing/duration/delivery method
   - Control type: placebo, active comparator, standard of care, historical
   - Blinding: open-label, single-blind, double-blind — justify choice

6. **Randomization plan** (if applicable):
   - Method: simple, block, stratified, adaptive
   - Stratification factors
   - Allocation ratio

7. **Sample size estimation**:
   - State all assumptions (effect size, variability, alpha, power)
   - Account for dropout rate
   - Provide the formula or simulation approach

8. **Statistical analysis plan outline**:
   - Primary analysis method
   - Handling of missing data
   - Multiplicity adjustment (if needed)
   - Sensitivity analyses
   - Subgroup analyses (pre-specified)

9. **Bias mitigation**:
   - Selection, performance, detection, attrition, reporting bias
   - Specific strategies for each

10. **Regulatory & ethical considerations**:
    - IRB/Ethics committee requirements
    - Informed consent considerations
    - Data safety monitoring board (DSMB) if needed
    - Applicable regulatory guidelines (ICH, FDA, MFDS)

11. **Timeline and milestones** (high-level)

## Output
Produce a structured study synopsis (~2-3 pages) that could serve as the foundation for a full protocol.
